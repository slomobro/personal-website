Enter the wilderness

Most of the internet is a lawn — mown, fenced, sorted newest-first, and arranged to keep you scrolling. This is not that.

A digital garden is a lovely idea: a place you cultivate slowly, where pages are tended and revised instead of published and forgotten. But a garden is orderly. It is weeded. What I'm making is a little more untamed — a digital wilderness. I clear a few trails and tend what needs tending, but I let the rest grow wild: half-formed thoughts, tangled essays, things I'm still learning out loud. You won't find a tidy timeline. You'll find a landscape.

Roam it however you like. Some paths are well-worn. Some end abruptly. Come back later and the undergrowth will have shifted.
